## Genre Prohibitions

- Day Zero that doesn't permanently change the world — no reverting to normal
- Early chapters without genuine survival danger — real stakes from page one
- System arrival with no in-world explanation (even a vague one)
- Ignoring real-world geography and consequences when set on Earth
- Factions that are binary good/evil — competing interests, not cartoon villainy
- MC becoming unstoppable too fast — power fantasy earned through survival
- Tech usage without addressing fuel, ammo, and infrastructure collapse
- System UI prose dump that replaces scene (pages of menus kills pacing)
- Resource abundance — if food/water/meds are ever easy, the genre is broken

## Faction Archetypes & Natural Conflicts

At least **3** factions should exist by ch 10, each with a recognizable worldview. These are starting points, not types to be played straight.

| Faction | Core belief | Resource they hoard | Natural conflict with | Typical betrayal |
|---------|-------------|--------------------|-----|------|
| Government remnants / Military | "Order restored from above" | Weapons, fuel, vehicles | Warlords (legitimacy), cults (ideology) | Classified pre-event info the public deserved |
| Survivor community | "We rebuild by hands we know" | Food, water, shelter | Military (autonomy), predators (defense) | A founding lie about how they survived Day Zero |
| Criminal network / Warlord | "Power belongs to who takes it" | Territory, slaves, contraband | Everyone (predation) | Someone inside wanting out |
| Cult / Awakened | "The System is divine / chose us" | Converts, levels, ritual objects | Reason, science, military | The doctrine conflicts with observed facts |
| Traders / Caravan | "Commerce survives regime" | Information, medicine, luxury | Warlords (tariffs), cults (heresy) | Selling both sides the same intel |
| Old-world scientists | "Understand the System to reverse it" | Knowledge, samples, hypotheses | Cults (blasphemy), military (security) | Experiment subjects without consent |

## System UI vs Prose Balance

The System is a **tool of character**, not a substitute for scene. Budget:

- **Full stat screen / menu**: ≤ 1 per 3 chapters, triggered by meaningful event (level up, class unlock, reveal)
- **Inline system line** (`[New skill: Shatter Palm — Rank F]`): use sparingly for punctuation, not transitions
- **Narrated system** ("the pain registered as a red bar flickering behind his eyes"): preferred — keeps prose alive
- **Novel-worthy system reveals** (new mechanic, hidden stat, admin message): these are scene events, treat like plot reveals

## Survival Grounding Requirements

Every 5 chapters must show at least two of:

- Food acquired, preserved, or lost (source named: canned goods / looted pantry / foraged / hunted)
- Water source and purification method acknowledged
- Ammunition count / melee weapon condition
- Medical supply state (named drug or wound care specific)
- Fuel / battery / light source
- Named real location (city, highway, landmark) for geographic grounding

Skipping all of these for 5+ chapters = survival tone lost.

| # | Check | Trigger action |

## Pacing Guidance

- Early (ch 1-15): survival, confusion, learning system. MC is weak vs low-tier threats. Shorter chapters (2-3k words)
- Mid (ch 15-50): growing stronger, claiming territory, faction politics. Power fantasy increases; survival becomes strategic
- Late: expansion, faction wars, world-scale threats. MC powerful but not unstoppable; challenges are existential/political
- Introduce new threat types as MC grows — zombies → intelligent predators → rival factions → interdimensional invasions
- Balance survival pressure with power progression — desperation early, strategy mid, leadership late
