## Genre Prohibitions

- "Tutorial town" syndrome — 50+ pages of killing rats before the real story
- New world identical to Earth with a fantasy skin
- MC treating new world's culture as quaint or inferior without narrative consequence
- No real consequences for cultural misunderstandings — fish-out-of-water must have stakes
- Pacing too slow in the "culture learning" phase — drip-feed rules through action, not lectures
- MC's origin world becoming irrelevant after chapter 3 — the contrast is the genre's engine
- Isekai truck or summoning ritual with zero personality — make the transportation event matter
- Cheat/gift treated as free lunch — every op ability must have a cost, risk, or social price

## Opening Information Budget (chapters 1-5)

Control information leak. Do **not** dump; drip-feed through scenes.

| Channel | Ch 1 | Ch 2 | Ch 3 | Ch 4-5 |
|---------|------|------|------|--------|
| Transportation cause | Event shown, cause hinted | One false theory | Partial truth | Bigger question opened |
| New world geography | Immediate surroundings only | Nearest settlement name | Region/kingdom name | Political map rough |
| Magic/power system | Phenomenon witnessed | Rule observed (no jargon) | First attempt/test by MC | First cost paid |
| MC's cheat/gift | Discovered accidentally | Tentative use | First limitation found | First backlash or social cost |
| Language barrier | Active obstacle | Partial translation aid | Functional but accented | Slang/dialect confusions remain |
| Homeworld callback | 1 sharp memory | 1 comparison moment | 1 skill applied | 1 grief/regret beat |

## Cheat/Gift Design (the transmigration bonus)

If MC has a special ability from the transition (translation, status window, inventory, strong body), it **must** have at minimum:

- **Hard limit**: capacity cap, cooldown, range, or knowledge gap
- **Visible cost**: fatigue / lifespan / relationship / reputation
- **Exposure risk**: if natives find out, consequences are concrete (bounty, worship, dissection)
- **Cultural misread**: the gift looks like X to natives, who are wrong in a way MC must manage

Without these, the gift becomes plot-breaking by chapter 10.

## Homeworld Contrast Engine

The genre's engine is the gap between worlds. Every 3-5 chapters trigger **one** of:

- MC applies a real-world skill (first aid, engineering, salesmanship, cooking) in a way natives cannot
- MC misreads native custom because of earth bias, and it costs them
- A native asks an innocent question that hits MC's unhealed earth grief
- MC realizes something about earth only by seeing its absence here

## Checkpoint Checklist

| # | Check | Trigger action |
|---|-------|----------------|
| 1 | Have we established a new-world rule the reader didn't know? | If no for 2+ chapters → add a rule-reveal beat |
| 2 | Has MC's cheat been limited, costed, or risked exposure this chapter? | If cheat used for free → add cost or cut |
| 3 | Any homeworld callback this chapter? | None for 3+ chapters → insert memory, comparison, or skill application |
| 4 | Language/cultural friction present? | If protagonist reads like native speaker → add a misunderstanding |
| 5 | Does a native character drive scene with their own goal? | If everyone is MC's reactor → rebalance |
| 6 | Tutorial-town risk: are stakes still "kill rats" after ch 5? | Escalate to faction/political/existential threat |
| 7 | MC's identity shift tracked? | Outsider → guest → participant → citizen arc must progress visibly |

## Pacing Guidance

- Opening: transportation event → brief real-world grounding → arrival/disorientation → first guide → first concrete goal
- Early: cultural fish-out-of-water drives comedy and drama (every 2-3 chapters)
- MC's real-world knowledge should apply in surprising ways — seed these early, pay off later
- Learning the magic/power system should feel like discovery, not tutorial text
- Relationship building with new-world characters grounds MC emotionally
- Clash between home culture and new-world values creates conflict without a villain
- Mid-to-late: MC's identity shifts from "outsider" to "participant" — track this arc explicitly
