## Genre Prohibitions

- Floors that are cosmetic reskins — each must present materially different challenges
- Floor difficulty that doesn't escalate predictably — readers must understand power requirements
- No clear summit goal — readers need to know what success at the top looks like
- Horizontal progression (going sideways) instead of vertical climbing
- Skipping floors without earned justification
- Boss fights won through deus ex machina instead of preparation and growth
- Reward creep (every floor drops a legendary) — inflation kills tension
- Info blackout — if readers never know what's above, anticipation collapses

## Floor Difficulty Ladder (milestone anchors)

Every N floors, hit a **milestone** that recontextualizes the climb. Use these anchors as default spine; customize for your tower:

| Floor range | Milestone marker | What changes for MC |
|-------------|------------------|--------------------|
| 1-9 | Tutorial floors | Learning rules; survival-mode; party forms |
| 10 | First gatekeeper boss | Reveals climber rank system; earns title |
| 11-24 | Specialization floors | Build identity, first real loss |
| 25 | Mid-mid cull | Many climbers die here; named rivals appear |
| 26-49 | Faction floors | Tower politics activate; alliances form/break |
| 50 | Halfway trial | Reveals something about the tower's origin/purpose |
| 51-74 | Compound floors | Rules from earlier floors combine |
| 75 | Last-mortal floor | Pre-summit gauntlet; something irreplaceable is paid |
| 76-99 | Summit approach | Information famine; survivors scarce |
| 100 | Summit | What the climb was for |

## Boss Information Budget

Each floor boss must be **prepared for**, not stumbled upon. Pre-boss runway:

- **2 chapters before**: boss existence hinted (carving, lore, prior climber's record)
- **1 chapter before**: specific mechanic foreshadowed (climbers examine arena, interview survivor, find prior party's journal)
- **Boss chapter**: tests floor's learned rules plus one surprise (prepared MC can adapt; unprepared dies)
- **Post-boss**: reward shown, cost paid, next-floor rumor seeded

Skipping the runway = deus ex machina even if MC wins through skill.

## Reward Economy

Prevent inflation:

- **Common floor rewards**: stat gain, minor skill, consumable — low ceremony
- **Boss rewards**: named ability or item — one per boss, with a drawback or cost
- **Hidden floor rewards**: rare, asymmetric (powerful but niche / conditional)
- **Cost ledger**: every major reward has a visible cost paid before receiving it (limb, companion, memory, time)

| # | Check | Trigger action |
|---|-------|----------------|
| 1 | Floor number consistent across index/chapter/truth files? | Reconcile |
| 2 | Floor's unique rule/biome/mechanic made concrete? | If reskin → add specific environmental/mechanical detail |
| 3 | Boss chapter? If yes, runway (2 prior chapters) present? | No runway → plant foreshadowing retroactively or flag |
| 4 | Reward given — does it have a cost? | Free reward → add cost or downgrade |
| 5 | Party size / composition consistent with prior chapter? | Quiet loss not acknowledged → insert beat |
| 6 | Summit progress visible to reader? | Stagnant for 5+ chapters → add ladder-position reminder |
| 7 | Rival climber tracked — advancing or visibly stalled? | Offscreen for 5+ chapters → surface |
| 8 | Rest/prep interval after boss? | Missing → breath chapter or end-of-chapter beat |

## Pacing Guidance

- Floor introduction: establish new environment, threats, and rules (1-2 chapters)
- Exploration and problem-solving: MC adapts to floor's unique challenges (1-3 chapters)
- Confrontation: floor boss / puzzle / trial testing what was learned (1-2 chapters)
- Advancement: reward, brief respite, foreshadowing of next floor (0.5-1 chapter)
- Early floors move fast (2-3 chapters each) to hook readers with progression
- Later floors slow down (5-8 chapters) as complexity and stakes increase
- The summit should feel like a destination worth the climb — seed hints throughout
