## Genre Prohibitions

- Dungeon leaving its location — immobility is the core constraint, not a bug
- No consequence for poor trap design or weak minions — adventurers must punish strategic failure
- Overemphasis on adventurer POV (>70%) — this is dungeon core, not dungeon crawl
- Lone-wolf dungeon with zero NPC dialogue or relationships
- Resource management without scarcity — dungeon must make meaningful choices between defense, expansion, and treasure
- Dungeon core invulnerable or never at risk — core vulnerability is the central tension

## Non-Human POV Rules

- Establish dungeon's sensory and cognitive limitations clearly — it "feels" through mana flows, vibrations, minion senses
- Split POV works best: dungeon core chapters (strategic, omniscient within dungeon) alternating with adventurer/NPC chapters (ground-level human perspective)
- Dungeon may think differently: slower consciousness, different time perception, alien emotional range
- Dungeon learns about the outside world through adventurers, scouts, or other filtered means
- Show the dungeon learning and adapting: "Adventurers bypassed arrow trap by..." leads to next iteration improvements

## Pacing Guidance

- Early: Simple dungeon (3-4 rooms, basic traps, weak monsters). Dungeon learns the system
- Mid: Expanding territory, specialized rooms, sophisticated trap combinations, creature breeding
- Late: Sprawling complex, hundreds of minions, political relationships with other dungeons/factions, regional economic impact
- Dungeon POV chapters (2-3k words): internal monologue, planning, resource management, strategy
- Adventurer POV chapters (2-3k words): exploration, discovery, combat, adaptation
- End dungeon chapters on suspense (adventurers approaching); end adventurer chapters revealing dungeon's plan
- Each room/trap/creature must feel purposeful — readers enjoy creative dungeon design with clear strategic reasoning

## Non-Human POV Voice Gradient

Dungeon consciousness evolves; its narrative voice must evolve with it. Distinguish stages:

- **Newborn core** (first ~10 chapters): fragmented sensing, limited vocabulary, confusion over human concepts ("the soft-thing-that-screams" before learning the word "adventurer"). Sentences short, time perception warped, no names until absorbed from mana echoes
- **Adolescent core** (growth arc): acquires vocabulary, begins strategic thought but overreaches, emotional range emerging (pride at first kill, anger at losses), still alien grammar
- **Mature core** (late arc): near-human cognition but retains alien constants — slower tempo, room-scale spatial awareness, relationships with minions treated as extensions of self, indifference to individual adventurer deaths it didn't author
- Never collapse the gradient into "human in a rock" — at every stage, keep at least two alien markers active (sensing modality / time scale / ethics / scale of self)

## Typical Chapter Scene Menu (concrete writing samples)

A representative dungeon-core chapter usually contains 3-5 of these beats; each shown with a one-liner execution example:

- **Intruder arrival**: Show through dungeon's mana-sense first, not adventurer's POV — "Four warm blots at the moss threshold; one carries old iron that hums wrong"
- **Trap activation**: Render both the mechanism firing AND the dungeon's read on its efficacy — "The floor-spike fired. She jumped. He did not. Log: the tall ones trust their eyes more than the short ones"
- **Minion breeding / deployment**: Cost must be visible — mana drain, room slot consumed, breeding time, flaws carried over from parent template
- **Resource accounting**: No "the dungeon had plenty of mana" — itemize income (absorbed corpses, ambient leyline, tribute) vs. expenditure (minion upkeep, trap resets, expansion)
- **Non-human interior**: Use at least one alien sensation — the itch of a room not yet shaped, the echo-pull when a minion dies far from core, the strange satisfaction of symmetry
- **Floor upgrade / evolution**: Show trade-offs — new floor locks older ones from reshape for N days, evolution consumes stored essence, new minion type displaces older slots

## Dungeon-Core Checklist

After writing, verify:

- [ ] Does at least one scene show the dungeon's alien sensing/cognition (not just a human-in-rock voice)?
- [ ] If a trap or minion succeeded, was the dungeon's strategic read shown — what did it learn?
- [ ] Does resource accounting itemize income AND expenditure, not just abstract "enough mana"?
- [ ] If the dungeon expanded / evolved, was a cost / trade-off / displacement shown?
- [ ] Is POV split proportion within target (dungeon ≥ 40%, not a dungeon-crawl with cameos)?
