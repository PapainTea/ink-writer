## Genre Prohibitions

- Chemistry that isn't earned — readers must understand why characters care for each other
- Love interest who doesn't drive protagonist's transformation
- Easy romance without real conflict — tension is mandatory
- Fantasy romance without HEA/HFN ending — readers feel betrayed
- Mislabeling heat level — communicate clearly in story setup; readers self-select
- World-building as afterthought to romance — setting must complicate or enable the relationship
- Love triangles dragged out without decisive resolution
- "Tell" emotions instead of showing them — inner feelings through action, not declaration
- Consent fog — pressure, manipulation, or dubcon framed as romantic; be deliberate
- Fantasy subplot that goes dark and stays dark with no romantic through-line

## Romance Stage Ladder (emotional progression)

Each stage must have **body** indicators (what a camera would see) and **behavior** indicators (what a character does differently). Skipping stages = "unearned chemistry."

| Stage | Body indicators | Behavior indicators | Exit signal |
|-------|----------------|--------------------|-----|
| 1 Attraction | Eye-track, breath hitch, flush they try to hide | Finds excuses to be in same room, watches when the other isn't looking | First voluntary vulnerability |
| 2 Tension | Lingering touch, quiet proximity, heightened awareness of the other's presence | Asks a personal question, gifts something small, defends them to a third party | First direct admission (to self or ally, not yet to each other) |
| 3 Confession/First kiss | Tremor, shallow breath, tears or laughter that surprises them | Stops hiding, crosses a line they set | New obstacle surfaces (external or internal) |
| 4 Conflict/Separation | Physical withdrawal, sleeplessness, appetite loss | Sharp words, distance, protective or destructive act | Character growth choice made alone |
| 5 Reunion/Claim | Grounded touch (forehead, held hand), stillness instead of heat | Words matching actions; public or ritual commitment | HEA/HFN earned |

**Rule**: at least **one** body indicator per romantic scene. Emotion-label-only scenes ("she felt drawn to him") fail.

## Romance/Fantasy Weight Split

Declare which you're writing, check each chapter against it:

| Flavor | Romance share | Plot share | Rule of thumb |
|--------|---------------|-----------|----|
| Fantasy romance | 60-70% | 30-40% | Couple is the story; plot exists to pressure them |
| Romantic fantasy | 30-40% | 60-70% | Plot is the story; romance is the emotional axis |

If a chapter has **zero** romantic beat, the next chapter must carry two. Conversely, a 3-chapter stretch of pure pining with no external plot → insert action/world beat.

## Heat Level & Consent

- Establish heat level in book_rules (sweet / steamy / explicit) and hold it. Don't escalate mid-book without arc reason.
- Consent is shown through active language and body — not assumed. Enthusiastic, sober, reversible.
- Dubcon / fated-mates / magical compulsion tropes are usable **only** when the text acknowledges the ethics onscreen.

## Language Conventions

- ✗"She felt a wave of longing." → ✓"Her hand was already on the door before she remembered deciding to go."
- ✗"He was so handsome she couldn't think." → ✓"She lost the sentence she was building."
- ✗"Their eyes locked and sparks flew." → ✓"He looked at her. She forgot the question."
- Avoid "delve/tapestry/intricate/magnetic" — AI-voice tells
- Dialogue carries subtext; on-the-nose emotional declarations work only at confession/reunion beats

## Checkpoint Checklist

| # | Check | Trigger action |
|---|-------|----------------|
| 1 | Which romance stage is this chapter in? | If unknown → mark in plan |
| 2 | ≥1 body indicator in every romantic scene? | Missing → add concrete physical detail |
| 3 | Romance/plot weight consistent with declared flavor? | Drift → rebalance next chapter |
| 4 | Heat level consistent with book_rules? | Drift → flag, don't publish |
| 5 | Consent shown, not assumed, in any physical intimacy? | Missing → revise dialogue/action |
| 6 | Love interest has own goal besides MC? | No → scene with their goal |
| 7 | External obstacle still active? | Resolved offscreen → surface new pressure |
| 8 | Last chemistry scene > 3 chapters ago? | Yes → insert a scene (tension/vulnerability/conflict) |

## Pacing Guidance

- Fantasy romance: heavy romance focus early, building to consummation around 60-75%, resolution after romance established
- Romantic fantasy: romance subplot woven through action plot, key romantic moments at act breaks
- Chemistry scenes every 2-3 chapters minimum — readers came for the relationship
- Sexual tension can heighten during/after action (adrenaline, relief, protective instincts)
- Break up long dialogue-heavy romance scenes with physical action or setting detail
- Enemies-to-lovers: opposition → forced proximity → grudging respect → attraction → surrender
- Second-chance: history adds depth and pain; show how characters have changed
