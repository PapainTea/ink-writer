## Genre Prohibitions

- Genre bait-and-switch — if you promise cozy, never introduce world-ending threats or graphic violence
- Conflict resolution without work — problems must not solve themselves
- Manic pixie dream characters who exist only to change the protagonist
- Nostalgic falseness — romanticizing a "simpler time" without substance
- Cruel humor — humor must be gentle, never at someone's expense
- Existential stakes masquerading as low-stakes — keep threats emotional, not apocalyptic
- Purple prose describing food/nature without advancing character or community arc
- Conflict-free chapters that are purely decorative — every quiet scene still shifts something

## Cozy Scene Sensory Palette

Every cozy chapter earns its atmosphere through **specific** sensory detail. Keep a rolling log per chapter: hit at least 3 across different senses.

| Scene type | Sight | Sound | Smell | Touch | Taste |
|-----------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| Baking | Flour dust catching light; dough rising | Oven tick, timer, knife on board | Yeast, butter browning, vanilla | Warm dough, dusty hands, hot handle | Crumb, crust, jam seeping |
| Gardening | Earth color shift after rain; named plant | Trowel on stone, wind in leaves, bees | Wet soil, bruised tomato leaf, compost | Loose earth, thorn prick, sun on neck | Dirt on lip, stolen pea |
| Neighborly visit | Teapot steam, worn armchair | Kettle, creak of a step, distant dog | Wood smoke, laundry soap, old book | Chipped mug rim, shawl weight | Honey, stale biscuit, tea gone cool |
| Animal companion | Fur in morning light | Purr, claws clicking, sighing | Damp fur, hay, clean animal | Paw weight, warm flank | — |
| Seasonal market | Colored stalls, handwritten signs | Bargaining, cart wheels, birds | Hot cider, wool, horse | Coin edge, wool fiber, crowded jostle | Cider, roasted nut, cheese sample |
| Craft / mending | Needle glint, thread tension | Wooden loom, scissors, fire | Beeswax, linen, dye | Needle prick, thread drag | — |

**Rule**: A cozy scene without at least one **specific named** sensory detail (not "warm bread" but "rye crust still ticking from the oven") reads as decorative, not inhabited.

## Stakes Ladder (keep stakes small and real)

Cozy stakes are **emotional + relational + material at human scale** — never world-scale.

| Stake tier | Example | Resolution should take |
|-----------|---------|----------------------|
| 1 Daily | Tea gone cold; cat sulking; muddy floor | Within scene |
| 2 Weekly | Neighbor's fence dispute; a lost recipe; shy customer | 1-2 chapters |
| 3 Seasonal | Harvest failure; heirloom broken; newcomer's secret | 3-5 chapters |
| 4 Arc | Found-family formed; grief metabolized; place claimed as home | Full book |

## Low-Conflict Rhythm (how to avoid static chapters)

Rule of thumb: **every quiet scene must shift one of — relationship / skill / self-knowledge / small problem / place-meaning**. If nothing shifts, cut or rewrite.

Recovery tactics when a chapter reads flat:
- Introduce a **mild external friction** (weather, a broken tool, an unexpected visitor, a missing pet)
- Let a character **withhold** something small — a cozy character who always volunteers feelings drains tension
- Plant a **seasonal/calendar marker** (bread starter going sour, garden frost date) that the next chapter will pay off
- Use a **found object** (old letter, dropped glove, recipe card) to seed a small mystery

## Language Conventions

- ✗"She felt a deep sense of contentment." → ✓"She realized her shoulders had come down sometime between the kettle and the second pour."
- ✗"The village was idyllic." → ✓"The baker had the lights on before the birds."
- ✗"He was grieving." → ✓"He still set two mugs out and put one away before the water boiled."
- Show the work: chopping, stirring, mending, weeding — craft verbs do the emotional work
- Avoid "idyllic / tapestry / resonant" — genre cliché flags

## Checkpoint Checklist

| # | Check | Trigger action |
|---|-------|----------------|
| 1 | Does this chapter shift at least one of (relationship / skill / self / small problem / place)? | None → rewrite or cut |
| 2 | ≥3 specific sensory details across ≥2 senses? | No → add named detail, not abstraction |
| 3 | Cliffhanger or apocalyptic stake slipped in? | Yes → soften to cozy tier; move external threat offscreen |
| 4 | Found-family / community arc progressing? | Absent for 3+ chapters → schedule interaction |
| 5 | Seasonal/calendar marker present? | Helpful for rhythm; insert if series uses one |
| 6 | Every character treated with warmth, even antagonists? | Cruel framing → revise |
| 7 | Ending tone gentle (peace / hope / anticipation)? | Cliffhanger → convert to soft landing |

## Pacing Guidance

- Chapter length: 2-3k words with space for reflection
- Chapter structure: quiet opening → small event or interaction → internal response/growth → gentle transition → soft ending (not cliffhanger)
- No cliffhangers — chapters end with peace, hope, or gentle anticipation
- Seasonal/cyclical structure works well (calendar-based chapter rhythm)
- Downtime scenes must still plant hooks, advance relationships, or build contrast
- Slice-of-life texture woven with an emotional throughline — pure plotless chapters risk feeling static
- Every quiet scene must shift something: a realization, a decision, an intimacy, a small loss
